Where's it going down?

Longlook Farm, a beautiful barn, bar, and lodge near Tilton, NH. It's situated on a whole ton of acres, it's got a great view, a pond with a rope swing, the whole deal.

You're going to love it, just like we did when we first saw it!

The bride

Originally from Bow New Hampshire, Marisa is a physical therapist by day and a Sherm, Jeff, and food lover by night.

She plays lots of field hockey and is constantly discussing all seven books and eight Harry Potter movies.

She is still waiting for her Hogwarts letter.

The groom

Jeff was born in upstate NY and spent his childhood playing lacrosse, video games, and running track. His favorite snack is peanut butter on pretty much anything.

"I know the husband is supposed to be the "rock", but Marisa has always been mine. She's helped me grow into the best man I can be. She is supportive, loving, and beautiful (of course). I still can't believe she's going to be mine forever!!

How they met

Marisa and Jeff met on Halloween night, 2005. Star Wars Episode III was in the theaters, The OC was popular on TV, and the White Sox won the World Series (bet you forgot about that!).

The guy Marisa called "Cornell Boy" for months just wouldn't give up, and she finally gave in and agreed to a date with him. He made mac and cheese with tuna and frozen veggies in it. He still does.

The engagement

Jeff tells the story:

We got engaged at Homewood Ski Resort in Lake Tahoe, CA. As usual, I winged it.

I got the ring the day before we left for our ski vacation, and even as the ski chair reached the top of the mountain, he had no idea what to do. Luckily, it was Marisa that prompted, "Hey, let's take pictures at this nice clearing area." Thank god for those Momma B photog genes.

After we snapped a few photos, with the incredible Lake Tahoe scenery in the background, I dropped to one knee. I started to stammer out the proposal I had been turning over in my mind, but before I could get even a few words out, Marisa completely lost it. You'll just have to see for yourself below, and read my post on the whole adventure.
